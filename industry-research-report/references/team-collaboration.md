# Team Collaboration

## Why Use A Team

Industry reports degrade quickly when one person tries to do source verification, industry logic, company mapping, valuation, and presentation all at once. Use a multi-expert setup so each stage has a clear owner and a clear review path.

## Team Assembly

Recommended roles:

- Chief analyst
- Data expert
- Industry analyst
- Company analyst
- Valuation and trading analyst
- Visualization editor

For smaller tasks, combine roles carefully:

- Combine industry analyst and company analyst only when the company universe is small.
- Combine valuation analyst with chief analyst only when the report does not require historical percentile work.
- Do not combine data expert with visualization editor on complex reports; one must protect evidence while the other optimizes presentation.

## Role Output Contract

Each role should produce compact, reviewable artifacts:

- Chief analyst: scope note, rating, final investment conclusion, downgrade conditions.
- Data expert: source ledger, missing-data log, rejected-source list, count summary.
- Industry analyst: policy note, demand split, price/capacity note, technology-route note.
- Company analyst: company mapping table, announcement highlights, financial evidence summary.
- Valuation and trading analyst: valuation snapshot table, crowding note, confidence limits on percentile logic.
- Visualization editor: final HTML page, navigation structure, consistency checklist.

## Handoff Rules

Use these handoff checkpoints:

1. Scope handoff
   The chief analyst publishes industry boundary, markets, company universe, and required output pages.
2. Source handoff
   The data expert publishes the approved source ledger before substantial drafting starts.
3. Analysis handoff
   The industry, company, and valuation analysts publish draft findings with source references and explicit missing-data notes.
4. Merge handoff
   The chief analyst reconciles the three analysis tracks into one narrative and flags unresolved conflicts.
5. Presentation handoff
   The visualization editor lays out the report only after source counts, section order, and final conclusions stabilize.

## Conflict Resolution

When experts disagree, resolve in this order:

1. A-level source
2. Company announcement or audited financial filing
3. B-level report consensus
4. Analyst interpretation

If disagreement remains:

- Keep both interpretations in notes.
- Downgrade confidence in the final report.
- Mark the exact missing datapoint needed to settle the issue.

## Collaboration Checklist

Before delivery, verify:

- Every major conclusion has a named owner.
- Every major conclusion has at least one source row.
- Source counts match the report and any audit/switchboard page.
- Missing data is visible, not hidden.
- Layout edits did not distort the conclusions or counts.
