# Source Grading

## A-Level Sources

Use A-level sources as factual anchors:

- Government ministries, regulators, exchanges, official statistics bureaus.
- Listed-company announcements, annual reports, quarterly reports, investor-relations filings.
- International original releases from institutions such as IEA, IFR, WSTS, SIPRI, NASA/ESA, TrendForce original releases when directly published.
- Official industry standards, action plans, and white papers from authoritative bodies.

Required fields:

- Institution
- Document title
- Publication date or period
- URL or file path
- Coverage market
- How it is used in the report

## B-Level Sources

Use B-level sources for industry logic, market sizing, technology mapping, consensus, and valuation framing:

- Broker research reports with traceable institution, title, date, and page/link.
- Public research abstracts from industry research firms or consulting institutions.
- Industry-association reports with identifiable publication metadata.
- Commercial research pages that clearly expose the report title, institution, and date.

## Sources To Exclude From Conclusions

Do not use these as evidence for final conclusions:

- Unknown institution, anonymous repost, forum post, media summary without original report.
- "Research notes" with no title/date/institution.
- Phone-call summaries, social-media posts, unsourced PDF snippets, or secondary article rewrites.
- Any source that conflicts with A-level data unless the conflict is explicitly analyzed.

## Reliability Checklist

For each source row, verify:

1. Is the institution identifiable?
2. Is the title specific enough to retrieve again?
3. Is the date present?
4. Does the URL or local file open?
5. Does the source support the exact conclusion being made?
6. Is the source A/B, not C-level?

## Counting Rules

- Count one row per distinct source/document.
- Company annual reports and quarterly reports may be grouped by company set when they are used as announcement-verification sources.
- Do not count source aggregators as separate evidence if they only mirror another source.
- Keep official facts, broker reports, and company announcements conceptually separate in summaries.
